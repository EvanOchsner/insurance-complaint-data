# MI DIFS — submission guide

Snapshot of DIFS's FOIA submission rules, taken from
<https://www.michigan.gov/difs/legal/foia> on 2026-05-05. Verify before submitting in case the page has changed.

## Statute

Michigan Freedom of Information Act, **MCL 15.231 et seq.** (also referenced as MCL 15.241 et seq. on the DIFS page header).

## Submission channels

## Response timeline

- **Initial response: 5 business days** after receipt. DIFS will reply with one of:
  - granted,
  - denied,
  - denied in part,
  - or notification of an extension of up to **10 additional days**.
- If granted, DIFS either includes the records or states approximately when they'll arrive.

## Fees

- DIFS may charge for "search," "deletion / separation of exempt information," or "providing copies" if the cost would otherwise be "unreasonably high" — itemized in advance per FOIA section 4.
- **If the estimated fee exceeds $50, DIFS may require up to a 50% deposit** before processing the request.
- The current request includes a public-interest fee waiver argument and a $50 cap, with explicit consent to receive a written estimate first.

## Appeals

A denial can be appealed to the DIFS Director or filed as a civil action in the Michigan Court of Claims. If the agency acts arbitrarily or capriciously, the court may award attorney fees, costs, and punitive damages. Not anticipated for this request.
